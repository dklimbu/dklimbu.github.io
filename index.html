Hi. I'm Dil Limbu.

Experienced and highly self-motivated Ph.D. Scholar seeking to deepen knowledge of material modeling by completing independent/collaborative research projects focused on complex functional materials. Experienced in using density functional theory calculations for structural modeling and exploring the relationship in material phenomena, with expertise in Fortran/ Python programming, High-Performance Computing (HPC), and machine learning algorithms in current research in materials science.

Here's all the stuff I do.

Computational Modeling

High-throughput computing and statistical learning methods to understand disorder materials via computationally generated models and design complex functional materials.

Electronic Structure Calculations

Density functional theory (DFT) calculations are used to simulate the materials properties of quantum materials and calculated ground/excited state properties of materials to the gain a quantitative understanding of atomic-scale phenomena.

Machine Learning

Machine learning tool focuses on building applications for automatically and periodically learn and improve to predict quantum mechanical properties of atomistic systems at accelerating scales.
